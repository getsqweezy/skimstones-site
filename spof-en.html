A systemic approach focused on decision-making and resilience

A single weak point can shut down an entire system.

SKIMSTONES identifies, prioritises and secures Single Points of Failure across energy plants, thermal networks and their critical interfaces.

The challenge is not only to identify vulnerabilities, but to turn diffuse technical risk into clear, actionable and manageable decision priorities.

Today, operators must simultaneously guarantee:

  • Plant availability
  • Quality of service
  • Risk control
  • Compliance with increasingly stringent contractual and regulatory requirements

What SKIMSTONES analyses

Critical failures do not arise solely from visible equipment. They also emerge from interfaces, support systems, functional dependencies and gaps between documentation and operational reality.

Full industrial scope

The analysis covers the plant, the network, coupling interfaces, support systems and critical customers up to the contractual delivery point.

Cross-functional dependency reading

We examine hidden links between equipment, utilities, organisation, system architecture and actual service continuity.

Energy plants
conventional production assets and associated systems
Thermal networks
distribution, coupling points and service continuity
Critical interfaces
handover points, interdependencies and weak links
Support systems
utilities, auxiliaries, documentation and field operations

What sets us apart

Our approach combines dual expertise in industrial processes and systems architecture, a cross-functional reading of dependencies, and close attention to real operator conditions in the field.

Dual expertise

Process analysis, systems architecture and operational vision are combined in one diagnostic and prioritisation method.

Decision-oriented method

The goal is not a simple list of risks, but a decision-ready basis to strengthen resilience across the studied scope.
